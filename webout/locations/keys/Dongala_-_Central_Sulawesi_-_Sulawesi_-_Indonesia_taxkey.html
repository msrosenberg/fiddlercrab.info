1.
a. Claws are asymmetric, with one very large and one small (male). — Go to 2
b. Claws are small and symmetric (female). — Go to 4
2.
a. Eyestalks of crab are close together (a.k.a. "narrow-front") [footnote 1]. — ♂ Male Tubuca coarctata, ♂ Male Tubuca demani, ♂ Male Tubuca dussumieri, ♂ Male Tubuca forcipata, ♂ Male Gelasimus jocelynae, ♂ Male Gelasimus tetragonon, or ♂ Male Gelasimus vocans
b. Eyestalks of crab are separated (a.k.a. "broad-front") [footnote 1]. — Go to 3
3.
a. Major cheliped is unspeckled. — ♂ Male Austruca annulipes or ♂ Male Austruca perplexa
b. Major cheliped is speckled. — ♂ Male Austruca triangularis
4.
a. Eyestalks of crab are close together (a.k.a. "narrow-front") [footnote 1]. — ♀ Female Tubuca coarctata, ♀ Female Tubuca demani, ♀ Female Tubuca dussumieri, ♀ Female Tubuca forcipata, ♀ Female Gelasimus jocelynae, ♀ Female Gelasimus tetragonon, or ♀ Female Gelasimus vocans
b. Eyestalks of crab are separated (a.k.a. "broad-front") [footnote 1]. — ♀ Female Austruca annulipes, ♀ Female Austruca perplexa, or ♀ Female Austruca triangularis

Footnotes

  1. The front of a fiddler crab refers to the extension of the carapace between the base of the eyestalks; front breadth therefore refers to the distance between the base of the eyestalks. Many species have the eyestalks quite close-together (“narrow-fronts”) while others have them well separated (“broad-fronts”). In most species this is reasonable clear, but individuals in some may appear on the border. Also, the breadth can change as crabs get larger. There is variation within species, and the breadth of the front changes as crabs get older and larger (e.g., within broad-front species, the front gradually gets wider as the crab ages, with smaller/younger crabs having narrower fronts than larger/older crabs).
